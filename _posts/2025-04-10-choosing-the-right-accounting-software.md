One of the most common questions we get from business owners is: which accounting software should I be using? The honest answer is — it depends. The right software for a sole trader is very different from what a growing company with employees and multiple revenue streams needs.

## The main options available

**QuickBooks Online**
The most widely used cloud-based accounting platform in the world. It works in your browser, syncs across devices, and connects to your bank automatically. It's well-suited for most small to medium-sized businesses.

At DavisSon & Associates, we are an authorised QuickBooks partner and offer discounted subscriptions — so if QuickBooks Online is the right fit for you, you can access it at a lower cost through us.

**Desktop (non-cloud) accounting packages**
Some businesses prefer to keep their financial data on their own computers rather than in the cloud. This is a perfectly valid choice — particularly for businesses with limited internet connectivity or those with strong data privacy preferences.

## How to choose

Ask yourself these questions:

**1. How many people need access to the accounts?**
If it's just you or one person, a desktop package works fine. If multiple team members or your accountant need access at the same time, cloud-based is much easier.

**2. Do you have employees?**
If yes, you need software that handles payroll — or that connects to a payroll service.

**3. What's your budget?**
Cloud platforms charge a monthly subscription. Desktop packages are usually a one-time purchase. Over several years, desktop can be cheaper — but cloud platforms are generally more feature-rich.

## Why setup matters as much as choice

Whichever platform you choose, how it's set up makes a significant difference. A chart of accounts that's poorly structured means your reports won't give you meaningful information — even if the software itself is excellent.
